CHAPTER 02
Traffic Lights & Traffic Signs Mastery
★★★
Approximately 10–12 questions on the written test

2-1. Core Principle of Signals — Priority

According to the Road Traffic Act (Art. 5), all road users must follow the signals of traffic safety facilities. Signal violation is one of the 12 Major Violations and can result in criminal punishment.

💡 FREQUENTLY TESTED
When a traffic light and a police officer's hand signal differ? → Hand signal takes priority (Art. 5, Para. 2)

Police assistants (Enforcement Decree Art. 6): Exemplary drivers, military police, fire officials
⚠️ Construction site volunteers and highway safety patrol officers are NOT police assistants

2-2. Vehicle Traffic Lights (Enforcement Rules [Appendix 2])

No Left Turn on Green Light

A green light means "go straight or turn right." Turning left on green at an intersection without an unprotected left turn sign is a signal violation.

📜 COURT PRECEDENT (Supreme Court 2004do5848)
At an intersection with a three-color signal (green, yellow, red) and no unprotected left turn or U-turn sign, left turns or U-turns by vehicles are not permitted.

Right Turn on Red ⚠️

Unlike many countries, Korea allows right turns on red (RTOR: Right Turn On Red). In Europe and Japan, all directions are prohibited on red.

⚠️ HOW TO TURN RIGHT ON RED
1. Must make a temporary stop before the stop line, crosswalk, or intersection
2. Must not obstruct other vehicles' traffic
3. If no pedestrians, you may pass through the crosswalk even if the pedestrian signal is green (2022 revision)

Exception: If the right-turn 3-color signal is red, right turn is NOT allowed! ([Appendix 2] Note 3)

Yellow Light

You must stop before the stop line. If you have already entered the intersection, pass through quickly. Forcing entry on a yellow light is a signal violation.

2-5. Bicycle Traffic Lights

Where bicycle traffic lights are installed, follow those signals. If none, follow the vehicle traffic light ([Appendix 2] Note 1). At bicycle crossings without a dedicated signal, follow the pedestrian traffic light (Note 2).

Traffic Signs are signs or road markings that display caution, regulation, instruction, etc., necessary for traffic safety (Art. 2, Item 16).

CHAPTER 03
Intersection Navigation
★★★
Left/right turn methods, yield rules, Roundabouts

3-1. Left Turn Method (Art. 25, Para. 2)

📜 LEGAL TEXT
"When making a left turn at an intersection, all vehicle drivers shall drive slowly along the center line in advance and make the left turn using the inner side of the intersection center. However, where the city/provincial police commissioner has designated as especially necessary due to intersection conditions, vehicles may pass through the outer side of the intersection center."

In simple terms: Move to the lane closest to the center line in advance, drive slowly, and turn left using the inner side of the intersection center.

Left turn: Move to center line lane in advance → Use inner side of intersection center

3-2. Right Turn Method (Art. 25, Para. 1)

📜 LEGAL TEXT
"When making a right turn at an intersection, all vehicle drivers shall drive slowly along the right edge of the road in advance and make the right turn. In this case, the driver making a right turn shall pay attention to pedestrians or bicycles that are stopping or proceeding according to signals."

3-3. Uncontrolled Intersection — Yield Rules (Art. 26)

ParagraphSituationYield Rule
Para. 1Vehicle already in intersection vs. entering vehicleVehicle already in has priority
Para. 2Wide road vs. narrow roadWide road vehicle has priority
Para. 3Same width, entering simultaneouslyYield to vehicle from the right
Para. 4Left turn vs. going straight/right turnStraight/right turn has priority
💡 MEMORY AID
"Already in > Wider road > Vehicle from right > Straight/right turn over left turn"

3-4. Roundabout (Art. 25-2)

✅ ROUNDABOUT KEY RULES
• Travel counterclockwise
• Slow Driving or Temporary Stop when entering
• Yield to vehicles already inside
• Traveling in the opposite direction = violation (Supreme Court 2017do9392)

3-5. Slow Driving / Temporary Stop Locations (Art. 31)

CategoryLocation
Slow DrivingUncontrolled intersection / Curved section / Hilltop / Steep downhill / Designated by traffic signs
Temporary StopIntersection where left/right visibility is poor or traffic is heavy / Designated by traffic signs

3-6. No Blocking Intersections (Art. 25, Para. 5)

If the road ahead is congested and you would have to stop inside the intersection, thereby obstructing other vehicles, you must not enter that intersection.

⚠️ If a different speed is designated by traffic signs, follow the speed indicated on the traffic sign.

4-2. Slow Driving

Slow Driving = driving at a speed slow enough to stop immediately (Art. 2, Item 28). It is NOT a specific number like "XX km/h." Traffic safety facilities use the word "Slow".

4-3. Safe Following Distance (Art. 19)

You must maintain enough distance so you can avoid a collision if the vehicle ahead suddenly stops. The faster the speed and the worse the weather, the greater the distance needed. At 100 km/h on an expressway, approximately 100m or more is required.

4-4. No-Overtaking Zones (Art. 22, Para. 3)

⚠️ EXACT LEGAL PROVISIONS (4 Items)
Item 1. Intersections
Item 2. Inside tunnels
Item 3. On bridges
Item 4. Curved roads, near hilltops on slopes, or steep downhill sections, and other places designated by the city/provincial police commissioner with traffic signs

Overtaking must be done on the left side of the vehicle ahead (Art. 21). When the vehicle ahead is positioned near the center line to turn left, overtaking on the right side is permitted.

CHAPTER 05
Pedestrian Protection
★★★
Core value of Korea's Road Traffic Act — Violating pedestrian protection = 12 Major Violations

5-1. Pedestrian Traffic Methods (Art. 8, Art. 10)

Road with sidewalk: Pedestrians walk on the sidewalk.
Road without sidewalk: Walk on the road shoulder or the left edge of the road (Art. 8). If the left side is dangerous, the right side is also acceptable.
Crossing: Where there is a crosswalk, pedestrians must use the crosswalk (Art. 10).

5-2. Pedestrian Protection at Crosswalks (Art. 27)

Core Principle — Must stop when pedestrians are crossing

SituationArticleDriver's Obligation
Pedestrian crossing at a signalized crosswalkArt. 27, Para. 1Temporary Stop
Pedestrian crossing or about to cross at unsignalized crosswalkArt. 27, Para. 6Temporary Stop
Pedestrian crossing road without crosswalkArt. 27, Para. 5Maintain safe distance and Temporary Stop
⚠️ CONSEQUENCES OF PEDESTRIAN PROTECTION VIOLATION
Violating pedestrian protection obligations at a crosswalk and causing an accident constitutes one of the 12 Major Violations under the Act on Special Cases Concerning the Settlement of Traffic Accidents. Criminal punishment applies regardless of insurance coverage.

5-3. No Sidewalk Encroachment (Art. 13)

Vehicles must not encroach on sidewalks. Sidewalk encroachment is a 12 Major Violation. When unavoidably crossing a sidewalk to enter a building, you must make a Temporary Stop just before the sidewalk and ensure you do not obstruct pedestrian traffic.

5-4. Children's Protection Zone — School Zone (Art. 12)

Designated around elementary schools, kindergartens, daycare centers, etc.

ItemDetails
Max Speed30 km/h (Joint Ministerial Ordinance Art. 9)
Parking and StoppingProhibited in areas designated by traffic signs
In case of accident12 Major Violations → Criminal punishment
Fines / Penalty points (08:00–20:00)Up to double the normal road rate

5-5. Elderly / Disabled Protection Zone (Art. 12-2)

Designated within 300m around elderly welfare facilities, disabled welfare facilities, parks, sports facilities, etc. Like Children's Protection Zones, 30 km/h or less and double fines/penalty points apply.

5-6. Pedestrian-Only Road / Pedestrian-Priority Road

Pedestrian-Only Road: Road exclusively for pedestrians (Art. 2, Item 31). Vehicle traffic prohibited in principle.
Pedestrian-Priority Road: Road without separated carriageway/sidewalk where pedestrians have priority (Art. 2, Item 31-2). Vehicle speed limited to 30 km/h or less.

CHAPTER 06
Drunk Driving and Penalties
★★
Memorize the exact threshold values strengthened in 2019

6-1. Drunk Driving Prohibition (Art. 44)

No person shall drive a motor vehicle while intoxicated. Since it says "no person," it applies regardless of whether you have a license.

📜 LEGAL TEXT (Art. 44, Para. 4)
"The standard for being intoxicated as referred to in Paragraph 1, which prohibits driving, shall be when the driver's Blood Alcohol Concentration (BAC) is 0.03% or higher."

6-2. Penalty Thresholds

2019 revision: threshold lowered from 0.05% → 0.03% (1–2 shots of soju can exceed it)

6-3. BAC Testing and Refusal

Police officers may measure intoxication via breath test, and drivers must comply (Art. 44, Para. 2). If the driver disputes the result, a blood test for re-measurement is available (driver's consent required).

⚠️ REFUSING A BAC TEST
1–5 years imprisonment or 5 million – 20 million KRW fine + License Revoked
Refusing does NOT help avoid punishment! You actually receive heavier penalties.

6-4. Scope of Drunk Driving

Drunk driving applies not only on roads but also in parking lots and other off-road locations. Even driving 30 cm constitutes drunk driving (Supreme Court 2007do678). Starting the engine and completing the departure operation is considered the start of driving.

6-5. Drugged Driving (Art. 45)

Driving under the influence of drugs such as narcotics or psychotropic substances is punished equally.

CHAPTER 07
Emergency Response
★★
Post-accident procedures, yielding to Emergency Vehicles, railroad crossings, breakdowns

7-1. Actions After a Traffic Accident (Art. 54)

⚠️ CONSEQUENCES OF HIT-AND-RUN
• Fleeing after victim's death: Life imprisonment or 5+ years imprisonment
• Fleeing after victim's injury: 1–30 years imprisonment or 5M–30M KRW fine
• Even a minor fender bender — leaving the scene is hit-and-run!

7-2. Yielding to Emergency Vehicles (Art. 29)

When an Emergency Vehicle (fire truck, ambulance, police car, etc.) approaches:

SituationAction
At or near an intersectionMove away from the intersection and Temporary Stop
Elsewhere (not intersection)Temporary Stop at the right edge

7-3. Railroad Crossings (Art. 24)

All vehicles must make a Temporary Stop before the crossing, confirm safety, then pass. When the barrier is down or warning bell is operating, entry is absolutely prohibited. Violation constitutes a 12 Major Violation.

If broken down inside the crossing: ① Evacuate passengers → ② Send emergency signal → ③ Move vehicle out

7-4. Breakdown Procedures (Art. 66)

Pull over to the right → Turn on hazard lights → On expressways, place a warning triangle at least 100m behind the vehicle. At night, place a red marker visible from 500m in all directions.

Seat Belt, mobile phone, Parking and Stopping, night driving

8-1. Basics

Right-hand traffic (Art. 13) / Left-hand drive / Speed in km/h. Drivers from left-hand traffic countries (UK, Japan, Australia, etc.) should take special care.

8-3. Parking and Stopping Regulations

Difference Between Parking and Stopping

Parking (주차): Continuous stop, or driver leaves and cannot depart immediately (Art. 2, Item 24)
Stopping (정차): Stop for under 5 minutes, driver remains in the vehicle and can depart immediately (Art. 2, Item 25)

Prohibited Locations and Distances (Art. 32)

LocationDistance
Intersection, crosswalk, railroad crossing, road cornerWithin 5m
All sides of a safety islandWithin 10m
Bus stop signWithin 10m
Fire alarm boxWithin 3m
Inside tunnels, on bridgesEntire section
💡 FREQUENTLY TESTED
Can you make a brief stop (under 5 min) at a "No Parking" sign? → Allowed (No Parking ≠ No Stopping)
Can you briefly stop at a "No Parking and Stopping" sign? → Violation

8-4. Night Driving (Art. 37)

When oncoming vehicles approach or following closely behind another vehicle → Switch headlights to low beam. Using high beams that blind other drivers significantly increases accident risk.

9-2. Skills Test Deduction Items

Deductions for: not wearing Seat Belt before departure, not using Turn Signal, sudden start/stop, leaving the course, failing to stop at designated position, etc.

9-3. Road Driving Test Disqualification / Deductions

Immediate disqualification: Signal violation, Center Line encroachment, causing an accident
Deductions: Not wearing Seat Belt, not using Turn Signal, not performing safety checks, speed violations

9-4. Top 5 Tips for Passing

1. Seat Belt — put it on before starting the engine to avoid unnecessary deductions
2. Turn Signal — activate it early for every direction change
3. Strictly observe speed limits — speeding causes large deductions or disqualification
4. Do not force entry on yellow lights
5. Check left/right, check side mirrors — it helps to turn your head exaggeratedly to show you are checking
