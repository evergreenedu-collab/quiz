CHAPTER 01

Written Test Guide & License System

License classes, foreign license exchange, test structure · cost · pass criteria

★ Essential basics before the test

1-1. Types of Driver's Licenses (Road Traffic Act, Article 80)

Korean driver's licenses are divided into Class 1, Class 2, and Provisional. Each class allows different vehicles.

ClassSubtypeVehicles allowed
Class 1
(Commercial · Heavy)		 Large Cars, vans, trucks (no weight limit), some construction equipment, special vehicles (excl. heavy tow/recovery)
Regular Cars, vans up to 15 passengers, trucks under 12 tons, special vehicles under 10 tons
Small 3-wheel cargo, 3-wheel passenger vehicles
Special Heavy tow trucks, light tow trucks, recovery vehicles
Class 2
(Personal use)		 Regular Cars, vans up to 10 passengers, trucks up to 4 tons
Small Motorcycles over 125cc
Moped (PM) Motorcycles up to 125cc, electric kickboards (PM), etc.
Provisional Class 1 / 2 Regular Temporary license required for the road test (valid for 1 year)
💡 Class 1 vs Class 2 — which to get?
If you only drive a personal car, Class 2 Regular is enough. The test is easier and the cost is the same.
If you need to drive trucks, 11–15 passenger vans, or for work, get Class 1 Regular.
⚠️ A Class 2 license cannot drive 11+ passenger vans (including the 11-seat Carnival).
⚠️ Important for Foreign Drivers — Automatic vs Manual
Korean licenses come in two flavors: Automatic-only (AT) and Standard (manual + automatic).
• AT license: Only automatic vehicles. Easier test. Most foreigners choose this.
• Standard license: Both manual and automatic.
If you already hold a manual license back home, getting the Standard license in Korea is the better choice.

1-2. Foreign License Exchange System

Foreigners who already hold a valid driver's license in their home country can exchange it for a Korean license without taking the written, skill, or road tests, if they meet certain conditions (Article 84).

Eligibility

  1. You hold a valid driver's license from your home country (equivalent to Korean Class 2 Regular or higher)
  2. The license is from a country with a license recognition agreement with Korea (varies by country/state)
  3. You hold a Korean foreign resident registration (F/E/D visa, etc.) or overseas Korean registration
  4. You can prove you actually lived in your home country when you obtained the license (passport entry/exit records)
⚠️ Recognition scope varies by country
• Full recognition (no test required): Japan, Germany, France, UK, Australia, most OECD countries, Taiwan — exchanged directly to Class 2 Regular
• Partial recognition (written test only): varies by country, please check with KoRoad
• Not recognized: must take all tests from the beginning

※ For 🇺🇸 USA, 🇨🇳 China, and 🇻🇳 Vietnam, recognition depends on the specific state/province — please verify.

Required documents

  • Original foreign driver's license + Korean translation (notarized or apostilled)
  • Original passport and copy (with entry/exit records)
  • Foreign resident registration card
  • 2 ID photos (3.5 × 4.5 cm)
  • Fee approx. KRW 8,000
  • Vision and physical test (at the license center)
💡 After the exchange
Your original foreign license is surrendered to KoRoad, and you can reclaim it when you leave Korea. The Korean license issued by exchange is valid only in Korea — for an International Driving Permit (IDP) you must apply separately.

CHAPTER 02

Mastering Traffic Signals & Road Signs

Vehicle / pedestrian signals, flashing lights, 4 sign categories, road markings — about 10–12 questions

★★★ Most frequently tested area

2-1. Core Principle — Signal Priority

Under Article 5 of the Road Traffic Act, all road users must obey the signals of traffic safety devices. Signal violation is one of the 12 major offenses, meaning criminal punishment regardless of insurance coverage.

① Hand signal (top priority)
▸
② Traffic light
▸
③ Road sign
📝 Key test points
Q. If a traffic light and a police officer's hand signal disagree, which wins?
→ Hand signal wins (Article 5(2))

Q. Who counts as a "police assistant"? (Enforcement Decree Article 6)
✅ Yes: model drivers, military police, firefighters
❌ No: construction-site volunteers, highway patrol officers, parking attendants
⚠️ Important for Foreign Drivers
In many countries, traffic-officer hand signals are used only in emergencies. In Korea, officers routinely direct rush-hour traffic by hand. If their signal contradicts the lights, follow the hand signal.

2-2. Vehicle Traffic Lights (Enforcement Rules, Schedule 2)

Korean vehicle signals are usually arranged horizontally: red on the left, then yellow, then green. Left-turn arrows may be added.

Green
Go straight or turn right
Left turn allowed only with an "Unprotected Left Turn" sign
Yellow
Stop before the stop line
If already in the intersection, clear it quickly
Red
Stop
Right turn possible after a full stop (with conditions)
Green Arrow
Only the arrow direction is allowed
No other movement

🟢 Green light — left turn is NOT allowed

Green light means "go straight or turn right." Many foreigners assume green allows any direction, but turning left on green at an intersection without an "Unprotected Left Turn" sign is a signal violation.

📜 Supreme Court Decision 2004Do5848
At an intersection with a red/yellow/green 3-color signal and no unprotected-left or U-turn sign, left turns and U-turns by vehicles are not permitted.
🌍 How other countries differ
🇺🇸 USA · 🇨🇦 Canada: Left turn allowed on green (after yielding to oncoming traffic)
🇬🇧 UK: Left turn only with a left-turn arrow (drives on the left)
🇨🇳 China: Left turn allowed on green
🇪🇺 Europe · 🇯🇵 Japan: Left turn only with a dedicated arrow signal
🇰🇷 Korea: Only with a left-turn arrow OR an "Unprotected Left Turn" sign

🟢 What is "Unprotected Left Turn"?

"Unprotected" means not protected by a signal. Where a sign permits it, you may turn left during a green (straight) signal — but only if no oncoming traffic is approaching.

🚫 Strict warnings
• Unprotected left turn is allowed only on green
• Unprotected left on red = signal violation (KRW 60,000 fine, 15 demerit points)
• In an accident, the unprotected-left vehicle is usually 80%+ at fault

🟡 Yellow light — "Stop or go?"

Rule: stop before the stop line. But if you are too close to stop safely (already in the intersection), clear it quickly.

⚠️ Accelerating into yellow is a signal violation. "Speed up because it's yellow" is a dangerous mistake.

🔴 Right Turn On Red (RTOR) — most important!

Korea allows right turn on red (RTOR), but only under strict conditions.

1
Make a complete stop — wheels fully stopped (0 km/h) before the stop line / crosswalk / intersection
2
Check pedestrians — if anyone is on, or about to step onto, the crosswalk, wait until they have fully crossed
3
Check other vehicles — do not obstruct any vehicle proceeding on its own signal
4
Turn slowly once all conditions are met
🚫 When right turn on red is NOT allowed
① Right-turn 3-color signal is red — at intersections with a dedicated right-turn signal, red means NEVER turn right (Schedule 2, Note 3)
② "No Right Turn" sign — no right turn under any signal
③ A car ahead is blocking the right-turn lane — honking a car waiting at the light = KRW 40,000 fine
🌍 Right Turn on Red — country comparison
CountryRTORNotes
🇺🇸 USA✅ Allowed (most states)NYC and a few cities prohibit
🇬🇧 UK❌ ProhibitedDrives on the left
🇰🇷 Korea✅ Allowed (after full stop)Stop rules tightened in 2023

📍 Real-world scenarios

🚦 Scenario 1: Red light, no pedestrian
✅ Correct: Full stop at the line → confirm no pedestrians → confirm no obstruction to other traffic → turn right slowly
❌ Wrong: Roll through without stopping → signal violation
🚦 Scenario 2: Green light, pedestrian on second crosswalk
✅ Correct: Cross the first crosswalk slowly → full stop at the second → wait until the pedestrian has fully crossed
❌ Wrong: "Green = OK" → pedestrian protection violation (in an accident, 100% at fault)
🚦 Scenario 3: Right-turn 3-color signal is red
✅ Correct: Stop at the line and wait for the green arrow
❌ Wrong: "No pedestrians, no cars, so..." → NEVER allowed! Signal violation
📝 Test essentials — must remember!
1. RTOR = mandatory full stop first
2. Right-turn 3-color signal red = NO right turn ever
3. Pedestrian green but no pedestrian? → After the 2022 amendment you may proceed (but stop if anyone is about to cross)

2-3. Flashing Signals

Flashing signals are mostly used at night/early morning or on minor roads where traffic is light.

Flashing Yellow
Proceed with caution
"Yellow flash = careful GO"
Usually on the major road
Flashing Red
Stop, then go
"Red flash = STOP then go"
Usually on the minor road
💡 Memory aid
Yellow flash = "caution" and proceed (no need to stop)
Red flash = "stop" first, then go after checking it's safe

⚠️ At intersections one side is often yellow flash, the other red flash. The red-flash side yields.

2-4. Pedestrian Signals

SignalMeaningPedestrian action
🟢 GreenMay crossYou may use the crosswalk
🟢 Flashing greenDo NOT start crossingDon't begin a new crossing. If already crossing, finish quickly or return to the curb
🔴 RedDo not crossStay on the curb
⚠️ Common mistake by foreigners
"Green is flashing — can I rush across?"
→ No! Flashing green means do not start a new crossing. Anyone already mid-crossing should hurry or return to the nearest curb.

For drivers: When the pedestrian signal is flashing green, do not move off immediately — pedestrians may still be in the crosswalk.

Pedestrian signals & vehicles (key 2022 amendment)

The 2022 amendment strengthened pedestrian protection:

  • Drivers must stop when a pedestrian is "about to cross" (standing on the curb)
  • At unmarked crosswalks inside school zones, drivers must stop even if no pedestrians are present
  • If no pedestrians, vehicles may pass the crosswalk even when the pedestrian signal is green
📜 Key articles
Article 27(1): If a pedestrian is using the crosswalk → temporary stop
Article 27(6): At unsignalized crosswalks, if a pedestrian is crossing or about to cross → temporary stop
Violation = one of the 12 major offenses (criminal liability)

2-8. Dedicated Right-Turn Signal & Reversible Lanes (NEW)

Dedicated right-turn signal (introduced in 2023)

Dedicated 3-color right-turn signals are being installed at intersections with frequent pedestrian / right-turn collisions. They are placed on the right side of the road.

SignalMeaning
🟢 Green arrowRight turn allowed (watch pedestrians)
🟡 YellowPrepare to stop
🔴 RedNO right turn allowed — different from the general RTOR rule!

Reversible lanes

Lanes whose direction changes by time of day. Always check the overhead electronic sign.

⚠️ Important for Foreign Drivers
In Korean cities, lane 1 (the leftmost lane) often suddenly becomes a left-turn-only lane at an intersection. To go straight, stay in lane 2 or further right. Always check the road-surface arrows.

CHAPTER 03

Driving Through Intersections

Left/right turns, yielding, U-turns, roundabouts, no-blocking rule

★★★ Most frequently tested area

3-1. Left Turn Method (Article 25(2))

📜 Statute
"When intending to turn left at an intersection, the driver of every vehicle shall approach along the center line in advance, slowly, and turn left through the inside of the center of the intersection."

In simple terms, three steps:

1
Move to the leftmost lane in advance — never change lanes at the last second
2
Wait for the left-turn signal — the green arrow
3
Turn slowly through the inside of the intersection center — do not swing wide
⚠️ Important for Foreign Drivers — Korean left-turn signals
Most Korean intersections use a dedicated left-turn green arrow. Free left turns on a green ball — common in the US and Canada — are NOT allowed in Korea.

Typical signal sequence:
① Left arrow → ② Straight green → ③ Yellow → ④ Red
(Some intersections reverse the order)

3-2. Right Turn Method (Article 25(1))

📜 Statute
"When intending to turn right at an intersection, the driver of every vehicle shall approach along the right edge of the road slowly, and shall pay attention to pedestrians or bicycles, etc. stopping or proceeding on the signal."
1
Move to the right lane in advance — turn signal on 30 m before
2
Check the first crosswalk — full stop on red, slow on green after checking pedestrians
3
Turn slowly along the right edge
4
Check the second crosswalk — full stop if anyone is crossing or about to cross
💡 Right-turn summary table
Vehicle signalPedestrianDriver action
RedanyAlways full stop, then turn after checking
GreennoneSlow turn
Greenpresent / about to crossFull stop → wait until they finish
Right-turn 3-color signal redRight turn prohibited

3-3. U-Turn Rules (NEW)

You cannot make a U-turn anywhere you please. You may only do so where a U-turn permission sign exists.

U-turn requirements

  1. A U-turn permission sign is in place
  2. The center line is dashed (crossing allowed)
  3. Either a left-turn signal or a green pedestrian signal (no pedestrians present)
🚫 U-turn prohibited
• Where a "No U-turn" sign exists
• Where the center line is solid yellow (crossing forbidden = 12 major offenses)
• Inside an intersection (unless the U-turn sign is at that intersection)
• On red signals
⚠️ Important for Foreign Drivers
🇺🇸 USA: U-turns are generally allowed at intersections (unless prohibited)
🇰🇷 Korea: U-turn only where a permission sign exists (prohibited by default)

Korean U-turn rules are the opposite of the US! Don't think "no sign means OK." Think "no permission sign means NO."

3-4. Unsignalized Intersections — Yielding Rules (Article 26)

At unsignalized intersections, four yielding rules determine who goes first. They are applied in order.

1
Already inside wins (Article 26(1))
A vehicle already in the intersection has priority over one entering.
2
Wider road wins (Article 26(2))
A vehicle on the wider road has priority over one on a narrower road.
3
Yield to the right (Article 26(3))
If road widths are equal and you arrive simultaneously, yield to the vehicle coming from the right.
4
Straight / right turn beats left turn (Article 26(4))
A left-turning vehicle yields to straight-going and right-turning vehicles.
📝 Memory aid
"Already in → wider road → from the right → straight beats left"

Common test pattern: "At an unsignalized intersection of equal-width roads with simultaneous arrival, who has priority?" → The vehicle from the right (Article 26(3))
🌍 Comparison with other countries
🇺🇸 USA: "Right of way" — first to arrive; tie → vehicle on the right
🇪🇺 Europe: "Priority to the right" (similar to Korea)
🇬🇧 UK · 🇯🇵 Japan: Drives on the left → vehicle on the left has priority
🇰🇷 Korea: Similar to Europe but in the order "already inside > wider road > right > straight"

3-5. Roundabouts (Article 25-2)

A roundabout is a circular intersection without traffic signals.

Counter-clockwise
▸
Slow / stop on entry
▸
Yield to vehicles already inside
💡 Roundabout key rules
• Drive counter-clockwise (Korea drives on the right)
• Slow down or come to a temporary stop on entry
• Yield to vehicles already in the circle
• Driving the wrong way = violation (Supreme Court 2017Do9392)
• No overtaking inside the roundabout
⚠️ Important for Foreign Drivers
🇬🇧 UK · 🇦🇺 Australia: Clockwise (drives on the left)
🇰🇷 Korea · 🇺🇸 USA · 🇪🇺 Europe: Counter-clockwise

Drivers from the UK, Australia, or Japan: the rotation direction is reversed. Be especially careful at first.

3-7. No Intersection Blocking (Article 25(5))

If conditions ahead would cause you to stop inside the intersection and obstruct other vehicles, you must not enter that intersection.

⚠️ In real Korean traffic
Intersection blocking ("kkori-mulgi") is common during rush hours, but it's a clear legal violation and frequently tested. Even on a green light, do not enter if the far side is blocked.

3-8. Bicycles at Intersections (Article 25(3)) (NEW)

Bicycles use a different left-turn method from motor vehicles:

  • Bicycle: stay on the right edge, slowly, and turn left along the edge of the intersection (two-stage left turn)
  • Motor vehicle: along the center line, through the inside of the intersection
💡 Bicycle two-stage left turn
① Cross the intersection going straight → ② Stop and turn the bike to face left → ③ Cross again on the new green
This is for cyclist safety.

"Frozen" = always 50%

💡 Memory: "Wet → 20%, frozen / blocked → halved"
⚠️ Important for Foreign Drivers
In some US / European countries this reduction is just a recommendation. In Korea it's a legal duty. Driving 100 km/h in heavy rain on a 100 km/h road is a speed violation, with major impact on fault assessment in any accident.

4-3. Safe Distance and Stopping Distance (Article 19)

Safe distance is the gap that lets you avoid hitting the car ahead if it suddenly stops. A simple rule: the speed in km/h equals the gap in meters.

SpeedReaction dist.Brake dist.Stopping dist.Recommended gap
60 km/h~11 m~22 m~33 m~60 m (dry)
80 km/h~14 m~38 m~52 m~80 m
100 km/h~17 m~63 m~80 m~100 m
120 km/h~20 m~80 m~100 m~120 m
💡 Easy memory — "speed number = meters"
60 km/h → 60 m, 100 km/h → 100 m gap
Wet road: 2× (100 km/h → 200 m)
Ice: 3× or more

💡 The 3-second rule: after the car ahead passes a fixed point, you should reach the same point at "one, two, three."
⚠️ Important for Foreign Drivers — rear driver = 100% at fault
Korea generally assigns 100% fault to the rear driver in a rear-end collision. "He braked suddenly" is rarely accepted. Keep a real safe distance.

4-4. Overtaking — Method and Prohibitions (Articles 21, 22)

📜 Article 21 — Overtaking method
① The driver of any vehicle wishing to overtake shall pass to the left of the vehicle ahead.
② A bicycle, etc., may pass to the right if the vehicle ahead is overtaking or about to overtake.
③ A driver being overtaken must not increase speed or obstruct the overtaking vehicle.

Overtaking summary

  1. Left turn signal on, check rear and side
  2. Check oncoming traffic (only relevant on 1-lane each way roads)
  3. Move left and overtake
  4. Once you have enough distance, return to the right
🚫 Overtaking-prohibited locations (Article 22(3)) — 12 major offenses
① Intersections
② Inside tunnels
③ On bridges
④ Locations posted by safety signs (curves, hill crests, steep downhills, etc.)

💡 Memory: "intersection / tunnel / bridge / sign"
🚫 Overtaking-prohibited targets / times (Article 22(1), (2))
• Overtaking an emergency vehicle → prohibited
• When the vehicle ahead is already overtaking → prohibited
• When the vehicle ahead is changing lanes left (e.g., waiting to turn left) → prohibited. In that case you may overtake on its right
• If oncoming or following vehicles would be obstructed → prohibited
• While a vehicle behind is overtaking you → prohibited

Overtaking method, time, or place violations are major offenses.
🌍 Overtaking — country comparison
🇰🇷 Korea: Left-side overtaking. Prohibited in intersections / tunnels / bridges / posted areas
🇺🇸 USA: Mostly left-side, but many states allow right-side overtaking on highways
🇩🇪 Germany: Strict left-side rule even on the Autobahn — right-side overtaking forbidden
🇯🇵 Japan · 🇬🇧 UK: Drives on the left → overtake on the right (mirror image!)

CHAPTER 05

Pedestrian Protection Duties

Crosswalks, pedestrian-priority roads, school / senior zones — 2022 amendment essentials

★★★ Major offense, frequent

5-1. Key 2022 Pedestrian Protection Amendment (Article 27)

The July 2022 amendment strongly expanded driver duties toward pedestrians. This is the area where foreigners make the most mistakes.

📜 Article 27 — Pedestrian protection
① The driver of every vehicle shall make a temporary stop in front of a crosswalk when a pedestrian is using it.
③ At an unsignalized intersection or its vicinity, when a pedestrian is crossing, the driver shall not obstruct the pedestrian.
⑥ At an unsignalized crosswalk, when a pedestrian is crossing or about to cross, the driver shall make a temporary stop.
⑦ Even outside ordinary roads (apartment grounds, parking lots), the driver must take care for pedestrian safety.
🚫 Key change — "about to cross" is now protected
Before: only pedestrians actively crossing were protected
Now: protection begins when a pedestrian on the curb is about to cross → driver must stop

⚠️ At a crosswalk, if a pedestrian is standing on the curb, you must stop even before they step off. Violation + accident = major offense.

5-2. School / Senior / Disabled Protection Zones (Articles 12, 12-2)

ZoneCoverageSpeed limitKey rule
School zone~300 m radius from elementary schools / kindergartens / daycares30 km/hNo parking / standing, mandatory stop at unsignalized crosswalks
Senior zone~300 m around senior facilities30 km/hParking restrictions possible
Disabled zoneAround special schools / facilities30 km/hParking restrictions possible
🚫 "Min-sik Law" — aggravated punishment in school zones
Injuring a child (under 13) in a school zone: 1–15 years prison or 5–30 million KRW fine
Death: life or 3+ years prison

⚠️ Fines and demerit points are doubled in school zones.
⚠️ At unsignalized crosswalks, you must stop regardless of whether pedestrians are present.
⚠️ Important for Foreign Drivers — never speed in a school zone
Korean school zones have some of the strictest rules in the world. A momentary slip past 30 km/h during morning or afternoon hours leads to large fines and demerit points. When your nav app announces "school zone," check your speedometer immediately.

5-3. Pedestrian-Priority Roads (Article 2(31-2), Article 27-2) — new in 2022

For roads where the carriageway and sidewalk are not separated, a new "Pedestrian-Priority Road" system was created.

  • Pedestrians may use any part of the road
  • Vehicles must keep a safe distance and slow down when passing pedestrians
  • Do not obstruct or endanger pedestrians
  • Speed limit: 20 km/h or less
💡 How to spot one
Usually narrow alleys without a sidewalk. Marked with road-surface paint or a sign.

5-4. How Pedestrians Should Walk (Articles 8, 10)

  • If there is a sidewalk → use the sidewalk
  • If not → walk on the left edge (so you can see oncoming traffic)
  • Use a crosswalk where one exists
  • Where there is no crosswalk → cross by the shortest path without obstructing traffic
🌍 Pedestrian fatality — Korea's reality
Korea's share of road deaths that are pedestrians is roughly twice the OECD average. Senior pedestrian deaths are especially high, making protection a top policy priority.

🇺🇸 USA: Lower pedestrian share, but fewer pedestrians overall
🇪🇺 Europe: Strong pedestrian-first culture; cars yield first
🇰🇷 Korea: Many narrow urban alleys → defensive driving is essential

DUI · Drug Driving and Penalties

★★★ 12 major offenses · severest punishments

6-1. Blood Alcohol Concentration (Article 44)

📜 Article 44(4)
"The 'state of intoxication' is defined as a blood alcohol concentration of 0.03% or higher."
BACCategoryPenalty
0.03% – 0.08%SimpleUp to 1 year prison or up to 5 million KRW fine, license suspended 100 days
0.08% – 0.2%Mid1–2 years prison or 5–10 million KRW fine, license cancelled
0.2%+Severe2–5 years prison or 10–20 million KRW fine, license cancelled
Test refusal—1–5 years prison or 5–20 million KRW fine, license cancelled
Repeat offender (2x+)"Yoon Chang-ho" Law2–6 years prison or 10–30 million KRW fine
⚠️ Important for Foreign Drivers — 0.03% is very strict
Korea's 0.03% limit is among the strictest in the world. An adult man can exceed it after just 1–2 shots of soju. "Just a little" is the most dangerous mindset.

🇺🇸 USA: 0.08% in most states (commercial drivers 0.04%)
🇬🇧 UK: 0.08% (England / Wales), 0.05% (Scotland)
🇯🇵 Japan: 0.03%
🇨🇳 China: 0.02% (stricter than Korea)
🇩🇪 Germany: 0.05%
🇻🇳 Vietnam: 0.00% (any alcohol prohibited)

6-2. Sobriety Test and Refusal

  1. Breath screening with an alcohol sensor
  2. If positive, retest with a precision breathalyzer
  3. If the driver disputes the breath result, a blood test is taken (with consent)
  4. Blood result is the final evidence
🚫 Refusal is also a crime
Refusing a police officer's sobriety test is a refusal offense punished at the 0.08–0.2% level (1–5 years prison, license cancelled). You can't dodge it.

6-3. Aiding · Drugs · Off-Road Locations

Passenger / aider liability

Anyone who provides a vehicle or rides along knowing the driver is drunk is also liable as an aider (Criminal Code Article 32 + Article 44).

Drug driving (Article 45)

📜 Article 45
"A driver shall not drive a motor vehicle, etc. when, due to fatigue, illness, or drugs, etc., normal driving may not be possible."

Covered substances include narcotics, marijuana, hallucinogens, and even cold medicine, sleeping pills, and tranquilizers. Penalty: up to 3 years prison or up to 10 million KRW fine.

Off-road locations also count

⚠️ Important for Foreign Drivers — DUI in parking lots too
Since the 2011 amendment, DUI applies even in apartment parking lots and private parking lots. "I just moved my car a little" is not an excuse (Supreme Court 2017Do17762).

6-4. Korea's Designated-Driver Culture

Korea has a highly developed designated-driver system. When you've had a drink, you call a "daeri" driver (대리운전) who drives your own car home for you.

  • How to use: smartphone app (Kakao T Daeri, T-map Daeri) or phone call
  • Cost: usually 15,000–40,000 KRW depending on distance
  • Flyers and phone numbers are everywhere outside restaurants and bars
💡 Foreigner-friendly
Kakao T supports English. Enter your car plate and destination and a driver shows up. Pay in app or in cash. If you live in Korea, install it now.

✅ Answer: ③ — Refusal is punished at the 0.08–0.2% level.

CHAPTER 07

Accidents & Emergency Response

Post-accident actions, secondary accident prevention, 12 major offenses, emergency vehicle yielding, breakdown procedures

★★★ Full 12 major offense list

7-1. Actions After an Accident (Article 54)

📜 Article 54(1) and (2)
① When a traffic accident occurs, the driver and crew shall immediately stop and take necessary measures to rescue the injured.
② They shall report to a police officer at the scene, or — if none is present — to the nearest police station without delay.
1
Stop immediately — leaving the scene can become a hit-and-run.
2
Check for injuries · call 119 — top priority if anyone is hurt
3
Prevent secondary accidents — hazard lights → triangle warning → evacuate beyond the guardrail
4
Call 112 — report to police. For minor mutual injury you may call your insurer first; police dispatch is optional
5
Preserve evidence — photos from multiple angles, exchange plate numbers and insurer info
🚫 Hit-and-run — the heaviest punishment
Fleeing after an accident invokes the Act on Aggravated Punishment of Specific Crimes:
• Injury + flight: 1+ years prison or 5–30 million KRW fine
• Death + flight: 5+ years to life imprisonment
• Body abandonment + flight: death, life, or 5+ years prison

⚠️ No exception for foreigners. Interpol pursuit is possible after you leave Korea.

7-2. The 12 Major Offenses

The Special Act on Traffic Accident Handling generally exempts insured drivers from criminal liability for ordinary negligence accidents. But the following 12 categories carry criminal liability regardless of insurance.

🚫 Full list of 12 major offenses
  1. Signal violation — light, hand signal, or sign
  2. Center-line crossing — solid yellow / opposing lane intrusion
  3. Speeding (more than 20 km/h over limit)
  4. Overtaking method, time, or place violation (intersection, tunnel, bridge, etc.)
  5. Railroad crossing rules violation — failure to stop
  6. Crosswalk pedestrian protection violation
  7. Driving without a license — none, suspended, or cancelled
  8. DUI (0.03%+) · drug driving
  9. Sidewalk intrusion
  10. Failure to prevent passenger fall — driving with door open, etc.
  11. School zone safety violation — injury to a child under 13
  12. Cargo securing failure (added 2017)

7-3. Secondary Accident Prevention (Highways)

After a primary highway accident, staying on the roadway puts you at high risk of being struck by following vehicles. Secondary accidents are about 6 times more deadly than primaries.

1
Hazard lights ON immediately
2
Move the vehicle to the shoulder if possible
3
Set up a triangle warning sign: 100 m behind by day, 200 m at night (highway: ideally 500 m)
4
All passengers evacuate beyond the guardrail
5
Korea Expressway Corp. 1588-2504 or 112
⚠️ Important for Foreign Drivers — don't sit in the car
Sitting in your car waiting for the insurer is dangerous. On highways especially, get out and behind the guardrail right away.

7-4. Yielding to Emergency Vehicles (Article 29)

SituationDuty
Emergency vehicle approaching at or near an intersectionAvoid the intersection and stop at the right edge of the road
Approaching elsewhereYield the lane (slow down or stop)
On a one-way roadIf yielding right is impossible, yield to the left
💡 What counts as an emergency vehicle
Fire engine, ambulance, blood supply vehicle, police patrol car, military operations vehicle, etc. Must have flashing lights AND siren active.

7-5. Breakdowns and Vehicle Fires (Rules Article 40)

  • Move to a safe location (shoulder), hazard lights on
  • Triangle warning sign: ≥100 m behind by day, ≥200 m at night
  • At night, also place a red flash signal / electric lantern 500 m behind
  • Engine fire: get out immediately → call 119 → warn following traffic
  • Railroad crossing breakdown: evacuate passengers → press the emergency alarm at the crossing → wave a flag or signal to alert the train operator

CHAPTER 08

Parking · Lights · Korean Traffic Culture

Parking rules, parking culture, headlight rules, horn use, navigation apps

★★ Daily life and exam essential

8-1. Parking vs Standing (Article 2)

📜 Article 2(24) and (25)
Standing (정차): Stopping the vehicle for 5 minutes or less, other than parking.
Parking (주차): Continuing to stop, longer than 5 minutes, or with the driver away from the vehicle and unable to immediately drive it.
💡 The 5-minute rule
Driver in the car AND ≤5 minutes = standing
Driver out of the car OR >5 minutes = parking
Even brief unloading counts as parking if the driver leaves the vehicle.

8-2. Where Parking and Standing Are Prohibited (Articles 32, 33)

🚫 Standing and parking prohibited (Article 32)
① Intersections, crosswalks, railroad crossings, sidewalks where divided
② Within 5 m of an intersection edge or street corner
③ Within 10 m of any side of a safety zone
④ Within 10 m of a bus stop sign or marking
⑤ Within 10 m of a railroad crossing or crosswalk edge
⑥ Where signs prohibit standing / parking
⑦ Locations designated by the metropolitan / provincial police chief
🚫 Parking prohibited (Article 33) — standing OK
① Inside tunnels and on bridges
② Within 5 m of fire facilities (hydrants, etc.)
③ Within 5 m of either edge of a road work area
④ Locations designated by the police chief
📝 5 m / 10 m memory
Within 5 m, no standing/parking: intersection corners
Within 5 m, no parking: fire facilities
Within 10 m, no standing/parking: safety zones, bus stops, railroad crossings, crosswalks

8-3. Korean Parking Culture

  • Reverse parking (back-in) is the default — for visibility when leaving tight spaces
  • Double parking: when no spot is free, you may temporarily park in front of another car. Always display your phone number on the dashboard and release the parking brake so the car can be pushed
  • Mechanical parking lifts: fold mirrors, align precisely. Mistakes cost millions of won
  • Valet parking: common at fancier restaurants and hotels, tip 3,000–5,000 KRW
  • Public / private parking lots: 1,000–4,000 KRW per hour, much more in central Seoul
⚠️ Important for Foreign Drivers — show your number when double-parking
Double parking without a number can lead to complaints and fines. QR-code / mobile number-sharing services (Kakao, T-map parking) are widely used. Set this up on your phone in advance.

8-4. Headlight and Light Rules (Article 37)

SituationLights to use
Night (sunset to sunrise)Headlights, marker lights, tail lights, plate light
TunnelHeadlights, marker lights, tail lights, plate light
Dark in rain / snow / fogHeadlights, marker lights, tail lights
Parking / standing at nightMarker lights, tail lights
Meeting an oncoming vehicleLow beam (no high beam)

⚠️ Important for Foreign Drivers — high beams
Using high beams that blind other drivers is a violation in Korea (Article 37(2)). Almost never used in cities — only on quiet rural roads. If another driver flashes their high beams at you, it usually means "lower your beams" or "danger ahead."

8-5. Horn Use (Article 49(1)8)

📜 Article 49
"While driving, the driver shall not, without justifiable cause, ... 8. sound the horn, increase speed, or otherwise act in a way that endangers others or causes traffic risk."

The horn should be used only when necessary to prevent danger. Using it because the car ahead is slow, or to push someone off at a green light, is illegal.

⚠️ The horns you hear in Korea
Honking is common in Korean traffic, but most of it is illegal. Horn-use fine: 40,000 KRW + 10 demerit points. Honking to push someone off the line at a green = 40,000 KRW.

Q19Color of the center line?
